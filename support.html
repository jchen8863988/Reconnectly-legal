1. What this page covers

This page explains how to get help for the Reconnectly mobile application (the “App”): installation issues, basic troubleshooting, feature questions, and how to report bugs. It also clarifies what is handled by Apple (for example certain purchase and refund flows).

2. Supported platforms & distribution

  • Primary distribution: Apple App Store for compatible iPhone / iPad devices running supported iOS/iPadOS versions.
  • Not supported here: Enterprise sideloading, jailbroken devices, modified OS builds, or unsupported OS versions (support may be declined if the environment is unsafe or non-reproducible).
  • Development builds: If you are testing a non–App Store build, include how the build was produced (for example Xcode / TestFlight) and the exact build number.

3. How to contact support

Email us at hello@familymedvault.com. Please include:

  • Subject line starting with [Reconnectly Support] (helps us triage).
  • Device model (for example iPhone 15; iPad Pro 11-inch).
  • iOS/iPadOS version (Settings → General → About → Software Version).
  • App version (from the App Store listing or the App’s settings/about screen if shown).
  • Language/locale you use in the App (for example English / Chinese).
  • Steps to reproduce (numbered list) and, if possible, screenshots (no sensitive personal information—blur names/notes if needed).
  • Expected vs actual behavior in one short paragraph.

Security: Do not send passwords, recovery codes, government ID images, full payment card numbers, or unrelated highly sensitive documents. We will not ask you for your Apple ID password.

4. Response times (individual developer)

We aim to acknowledge most emails within 5 business days and provide a first substantive response within 10 business days, excluding holidays and high-volume periods. Complex bugs may require additional investigation or an App update.

No SLA: Unless required by mandatory consumer law, we do not guarantee a specific resolution time or outcome.

5. What we can help with

  • App crashes, freezes, or unexpected UI behavior (with reproduction steps).
  • Questions about core features described in the App Store listing (contacts/reminders/notes workflows).
  • Local data questions at a high level (for example how reminders are intended to work), without requesting unnecessary private content.
  • “Restore purchases” guidance for App Store subscriptions (see Section 6).

6. Purchases, billing, refunds (Apple-handled items)

For subscriptions purchased through the Apple App Store:

  • Billing and refunds are typically processed under Apple’s policies and tools (for example subscription management on your device).
  • If your issue is “I was charged but don’t have access,” include your Apple purchase receipt details you are comfortable sharing (for example date/time and product name) and what you see inside the App.
  • We cannot modify Apple invoices, tax documents, or Apple ID account settings on your behalf.

Apple support: https://support.apple.com/ (opens in a new tab).

7. Quick troubleshooting checklist

  • Update to the latest iOS/iPadOS supported by your device; install the latest App Store version of Reconnectly.
  • Restart the device and try again.
  • Check Notifications permissions (Settings → Notifications → Reconnectly) if reminders are not appearing.
  • Confirm Focus / Do Not Disturb modes and scheduled summary settings are not blocking alerts.
  • Ensure sufficient storage space; low storage can cause instability.
  • If the App won’t open after an OS update, reinstall from the App Store (note: local data behavior depends on iOS backup/restore settings—see Section 8).

8. Data, backups, and reinstall

Reconnectly is designed as a local-first app. Reinstalling or clearing app data may affect locally stored information depending on platform behavior and your backup settings.

  • If you use export/backup features in the App (where available), keep exported files secure and only import files you trust.
  • We do not operate a first-party cloud restore service for core App data in the current version described here.

9. Support limitations (protecting users and an independent developer)

  • Support is provided for the generally available App Store build unless we explicitly agree otherwise (for example coordinated beta testing).
  • We may decline support requests that require processing unlawful content, violating others’ rights, or circumventing security or subscription controls.
  • We do not provide on-site support, phone hotlines, remote desktop control, or legal advice.
  • We are not responsible for third-party services (Apple, carriers, MDM profiles, VPNs, firewalls) beyond reasonable guidance.

10. Privacy when you contact us

If you email support, we will use your message contents and email address to respond and to detect abuse/fraud as needed. See the App’s Privacy Policy URL provided in the App Store listing for full details.
